It isn't clear that many direct correlations can be seen. I notice that if you choose smoke % for the Y variable append and Household Median Income as the x-variable, it seems that there are a larger amount of smokers in the lower median Income bracket. It isn't glaringly obvious, but it seems like a larger of cluster of states with lower median incomes have a greater % of smokers. Additionally, when you look at percent of smokers on the y-axis and percent poverty we see a similar correlation.

There seems to also be a correlation between Median Household Income and % of those Lacking Heatlthcare. The divide seems to be evident around the median income of about $55,000. It seems that those with a MHI of less than $55,000 lack Healthcare coverage. Again, it is not glaringly obvious, but quite a practial inference. With the exception of state Medical coverage programs such as Medicare, which skew this plot inherently, it is clear that the less income members of a household have, they are likely less able to afford private healthcare.

Another interesting, yet again not bizzare observation is that the majority of smokers seem to fall between the ages of 34 and 44. I would have thought we would have seen a larger amount of smokers in their 20's. So, I found this to be interesting.
